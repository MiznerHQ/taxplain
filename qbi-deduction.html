What is QBI Deduction (Qualified Business Income)?

Deduction
⚠️ Educational only. TaxPlain does not provide tax, legal, or financial advice. Always consult a qualified tax professional about your specific situation.
What it is

The QBI Deduction (Qualified Business Income) a deduction of up to 20% of qualified business income for self-employed people and pass-through business owners.

Who it affects & common mistakes
👤 Who it affects

Sole proprietors, S-corp shareholders, and partners in partnerships with qualified business income — subject to income limits for certain service businesses.

⚠️ Common mistake

Failing to claim the QBI deduction at all. It's one of the most valuable deductions for self-employed people, yet many miss it entirely because it's relatively new and complex.

What it means for you

If your taxable income is below $182,050 (single) or $364,200 (married, 2023), you can generally deduct 20% of your net business income. Above these limits, complex rules apply, especially for service businesses.

What to do right now
This deduction can be extremely valuable for self-employed people — it's like paying tax on only 80% of your business income. A tax professional is highly recommended for maximizing and correctly calculating the QBI deduction.
