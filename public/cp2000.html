What is CP2000 Notice?

IRS Notice
⚠️ Educational only. TaxPlain does not provide tax, legal, or financial advice. Always consult a qualified tax professional about your specific situation.
What it is

The CP2000 Notice a notice the IRS sends when information on your tax return doesn't match what employers, banks, or other payers reported to them.

Who it affects & common mistakes
👤 Who it affects

Taxpayers whose reported income, deductions, or credits don't match third-party records like W-2s or 1099s.

⚠️ Common mistake

Ignoring the CP2000 and assuming it will go away. If you don't respond, the IRS will automatically assess the additional tax.

What it means for you

The IRS found a discrepancy between what you reported and what was reported about you. This is a proposal to change your return — not a bill yet.

What to do right now
You have 60 days to respond. Don't ignore it — review the notice carefully and compare it to your records. If the IRS is right, you can agree and pay. If they're wrong, gather your documents and respond in writing.
